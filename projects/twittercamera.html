Camera and Media Editor Study

An exploratory study to understand whether the updated camera + media editor experience meets user expectations.

September 2021

My Role: Lead UX Researcher

Research Methods: Moderated 1:1 User Interviews

Team: Gurpreet Kaur, Designers, Product Manager

...

Overview

The Tweets Evolution team has been working on an updated Camera + Media Editor experience, bringing the best of Fleets composition capabilities and some new concepts into the Tweet Composer.

The new experience hopes to elevate media in Twitter to meet user expectations in 2021. This study sets out to understand those expectations.

...

Research Objectives

  • Identify customers’ current needs and goals with the Camera + Media Editor to drive an experience that increases adoption

  • Understand perceived value and interest of new design concepts

...

Methodology

I conducted 12, 60-minute in-depth remote interviews with light, medium, and heavy Twitter users.

  • Interviews were conducted remotely via Google Meet with only US users

  • Ages ranged from 18 - 65

  • Participants were Twitter users who have used Twitter or Fleets editor to edit their photos and videos

Note: Unable to show designs/ detailed research plan due to NDA.

...

Key Insights

  • The ability to add text, stickers, and crops were helpful for customers to express themselves and create meaningful content within Twitter. However, there is a chance to further improve this experience.

  • New Concept* generated strong excitement among users because it is unique to Twitter and gives users the opportunity to build empathy and stronger connections. However, educating users will be necessary when introducing this experience.

  • The ability to trim videos using both the handle bar and custom trim method was easy, quick, and useful. However, users had difficulty discovering the custom trim feature.

  • Initially, users were hesitant with the segmentation feature* due to the video watching experience feeling disconnected. However, after some time users understood it’s value and showed great interest in using it.

* Unable to show disclose names due to NDA.

...

Recommendations

Users are very excited about the Camera and Media Editor improvements. However, for these new concepts to be a success, we need to clarify the icons, educate them on New Concept*, and design our tools to be accessible so users can express themselves more creatively.

  • Connect icons to their action - Icons should be intuitive, however there are a few icons that are confusing to users which ultimately hinders them from completing tasks. Build icons that users can recognize and that illustrate their actions.

  • Provide an introduction to New Concept* - Because New Concept* is a unique product, users may not understand how to use it or see it’s value. Provide examples of different ways users can be creative with the product.

  • Design for people with disabilities - Inclusive design increases access. Without accessibility, we miss out on connecting with all of our users.

...

Impact

  • One of the concepts that I had championed for was launched 2 weeks ago to iOS users.

  • Along with launch, my other research insights are still being used to refine additional features in the Tweet Composer

  • Research findings were requested for presentation to broader Research team
